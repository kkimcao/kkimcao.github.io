Project ideas

Overview

The ultimate goal of this project is to create an attachment for a pair of glasses that utilises sensors. The sensors mounted will measure the distance from the user themselves, to an obstruction. When the user reaches a certain distance to an obstruction, the glasses will vibrate at a certain intensity (depending on the distance) signalling a hazard to the user. The closer the distance the stronger the strength of the vibration. This will enable the users to gain more spatial awareness and a better grasp of their surroundings/area. Ultimately, this system in turn will reduce risks and hazards associated that can endanger human life.

Motivation

This product is intentionally built to aid the vision-impaired people, in the efforts of adding an extra layer of protection against risk, hazards and injuries. The significance of the glasses will be to provide vision-impaired people with better sense of their surroundings when getting around on foot. A survey of 607 blind and vision-impaired people was conducted about their experiences as pedestrians, showcased that 1 in 12 Victorians have been hit by a vehicle or bicycle while walking in the past 5 years. Using this product will provide the user vital information of the their surrounding at hand, which is one step to closer to reducing further risks.

Description

The product can precisely detect things incoming that is within its range. The sensor assures the user that the distance to nearby obstacles is measured, regards of the transparency or lighting conditions of an object. The information collected is relayed to the user instantly through intuitive vibrations.

The plastic frame design is made to extend the durability of the product for different environment conditions. In addition, it protects the sensitive parts but also it is easily accessible for replacement or maintenance. It is also water resistant and can function in light - moderate rain.

The product includes a selection of two different ranges of detection; a 1 metre range or a 2 metre range. The option to toggle between the two different range is just with a click of a button.

The sensor will be a crucial component of the product which will detect and record the ambient distance in the surrounding vicinity. The sensors can detect high objects a cane nor a guide dog can warn against. It works by detecting obstacles in front of users at head level, then measuring the distance between the user and the obstacle itself.

In conjunction with the sensor is a small vibrating component, which will be attached in the product itself. The operation of the vibrating is fairly simple, once a obstacle has been detected, the user can be alerted through intuitive vibrations, notifying the person to safely move around any objects they may encounter.

The vibrating component works together with the sensor, emitting instant alerts in cooperation of the sensor itself. The closer the detected distance of the object results into a greater strength output of the vibration. The intensity and frequency of the vibrations increase when the obstacle is approached closer to the person and vice versa. Within an armâ€™s length, quicker and faster vibrations emit alerting the user to stop.

The product is rechargeable and can last up to 10 hours when fully charged. The device can be charged using any standard Micro-USB cable. Users will be alerted of the battery level through certain vibrations, once turned on:

  • 1 vibration indicates that the battery level is lower than 25%
  • 2 vibration indicates that the battery level is between 25%-50%
  • 3 vibration indicates that the battery level is between 50%-75%
  • 4 vibration indicates that the battery level is above 75%

Tools and Technologies

The design of the product includes tinted lens, plastic frames, rechargeable battery to power the device, a button attached to toggle between ranging modes, sensors for detection and measuring distance of obstacles and a vibrating component that will be attached in the glasses. The components of the product are small and compact so that it is light enough to be mounted on the glasses.

The sensors will be initially calibrated with the vibrator to transmit an accurate alert and visual representation for the user of their surroundings. This can be achieved by using a program to code the measurements and calibrate the connection between the sensors and the vibrating device.

Skills Required

Coding/programming skills are required to calibrate the sensor with the vibrating component that emits vibration at a specific intensity when reaching a specific distance. In addition, an electrical engineer will be required for circuitry and wiring of different components to make up the device to cooperate with each other. The hardware components itself are quite simple and easy to obtain, however the production of the coding may be a drawback.

Outcome

If the project is successful, this will be very beneficial to the vision-impaired. It will largely reduce the risks and hazards vision-impaired people encounter every day, protecting them against any physical injury. This development can improve the confidence, the independence and the overall quality of life for those living with blindness or vision loss.
